Wormhole — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-17

Wormhole is a peer-to-peer messaging and calling app. Messages, photos, and calls are end-to-end encrypted and sent directly between your device and your peer's device over a WebRTC connection. Wormhole does not have user accounts, does not run analytics or advertising, and does not sell or share your data with third parties.

What Wormhole does not collect

  • No sign-up, account, email address, or phone number is required to use the app.
  • Message and photo content is encrypted end-to-end and is never readable by, or stored on, any server.
  • Messages and photos are stored only in a local, on-device database — never uploaded anywhere.

What passes through the signaling server

To let two devices find each other and open a direct connection, Wormhole uses a small signaling server that relays only the technical information needed to set up that connection:

  • The room ID you and your peer choose to pair your devices (not tied to any real-world identity).
  • Your device platform (iOS/Android) and, on iOS, a push token used solely to wake the app for an incoming call.
  • WebRTC connection setup data (offer/answer/ICE candidates), which can include your device's network (IP) address — this is standard for any WebRTC-based app and is required to establish a direct connection.

None of this is written to a database or retained after your session ends — it exists only in memory while your devices are connecting, and is discarded on disconnect.

Network relays (STUN/TURN)

When a direct connection can't be established (e.g. due to restrictive NAT/firewalls), Wormhole falls back to public STUN servers (Google) and a TURN relay service (Metered.ca) purely to relay connection traffic. Message content remains end-to-end encrypted regardless of whether a relay is used, so these services cannot read your messages.

Device permissions

  • Camera & Microphone — used only when you place or receive a voice/video call, or attach a photo. Media is streamed or sent directly to your peer, never to a server.
  • Photo library — used only when you choose to attach an image to a message.
